The Beginning

The establishment of Mohamed Jamaluddin School in 1992 was a significant milestone for Gn. Fuvahmulah and its community. As the school opened its doors on November 3, 1992, local families and leaders rallied to support the new institution. The school was named after the revered Islamic scholar Sheikh Mohamed Jamaluddin (Naib Thuththu), reflecting the community's respect for educational and religious heritage.

In its early years, MJS started as a primary school serving children from across Fuvahmulah's wards (Dhadimagu, Dhiguvaandu, Hoadhadu, and Maadhadu). The community played an active role — from advocating for the school's creation to contributing resources — to ensure children had access to schooling on the island.

Early on, the school faced challenges typical of a growing institution: limited classrooms and resources meant classes had to be held in rotation, with over 400 students sharing just 12 classrooms by the mid-2010s. Despite these hurdles, the dedication of local educators and international partners led to key improvements. For example, a Japan-funded project in 2015–2016 provided a new two-story classroom block, relieving overcrowding.

Early milestones such as expanding to secondary grades by 2016 (allowing students to continue up to grade 10 at MJS) and shifting to a single-session schedule in 2023, marked the school's evolution. Throughout "the beginning" period, the strong collaboration between the Fuvahmulah community and the school set a foundation of support and resilience. This foundation helped MJS grow from a humble start into a "successful primary school" and later a full-fledged secondary school, fulfilling the island's educational needs while honoring the legacy of its namesake scholar.

Our Journey Through the Years

1992

Foundation

Mohamed Jamaluddin School is officially inaugurated on 3 November 1992, fulfilling the community's dream of a local school.

2017

Silver Jubilee

The school celebrates 25 years of service, marking a quarter-century milestone in education and community development.

2021

Infrastructure Expansion

A project to construct 16 new classrooms and a computer lab is launched to accommodate growing student numbers.

2021

New Hall Opened

A modern multi-purpose hall is inaugurated by the Vice President, expanding facilities for students and school events.

2022

30th Anniversary

MJS marks three decades of education, reflecting on its legacy while continuing to evolve with the needs of the community.

Our Legacy

Over the past three decades, MJS has built a proud legacy. It has consistently been one of Fuvahmulah's most successful schools, nurturing generations of students who excel academically and uphold strong values. The school's commitment to quality and holistic education has made it a pillar of the community, with many alumni going on to higher studies and contributing to society in diverse fields.

Looking Forward

Looking ahead, MJS remains dedicated to providing quality, value-based education for the future. The school aims to equip students with critical thinking skills and global perspectives, grounded in core values like honesty, loyalty, and moral integrity. Embracing innovation while preserving our values, MJS strives to produce successful lifelong learners who are ready for the rapidly changing world.
